# Visual Guide

This guide explains how to read each chart, what insight it provides, and how to present it in interviews.

## 1) `financial_trends.png`
What it shows:
- Top panel: revenue vs operating cost by quarter.
- Bottom panel: profit vs cash flow by quarter.
- Orange-shaded region marks the post-subsidy-reduction period (from 2022-Q2 onward).

How to read it:
- Compare revenue and cost spread to understand margin pressure.
- Track the bottom panel for immediate shock (profit/cash low point) and recovery slope.

Key message:
- Despite post-cut pressure, financial discipline restored profitability and liquidity momentum through 2023.

## 2) `forecast_vs_actual.png`
What it shows:
- Bars compare actual vs forecast profit each quarter.
- Green line shows forecast error percent by quarter.
- Dashed green line shows average error across the timeline.

How to read it:
- Bar proximity indicates prediction quality.
- Error line identifies stress quarters where model bias or volatility is higher.

Key message:
- Forecasts stayed in a controlled error band while actual performance improved.

## 3) `swot_priority_scores.png`
What it shows:
- Heatmap of SWOT dimensions vs intervention themes.
- Higher values indicate stronger strategic priority.

How to read it:
- Darker cells and larger values identify where to focus resources first.
- Use opportunities/strength scores to justify scaling actions; use threat/weakness cells to monitor risk.

Key message:
- Cost optimization, demand forecasting, and working-capital actions were consistently high-priority choices.

## 4) `intervention_impact.png`
What it shows:
- Actual profit line vs counterfactual no-intervention line.
- Green filled area is implied intervention lift.
- Quarterly uplift labels quantify impact.

How to read it:
- Widening gap between actual and counterfactual means interventions are working.
- Later quarters should show larger lift if execution matures over time.

Key message:
- Cross-functional interventions progressively reduced the subsidy shock and expanded profit recovery.

## Suggested 30-second walkthrough
- "The top chart shows the subsidy shock timing and financial recovery trend."
- "The second chart shows forecast quality and error control."
- "The third chart prioritizes actions with SWOT evidence."
- "The fourth chart quantifies intervention lift versus a no-action scenario."
