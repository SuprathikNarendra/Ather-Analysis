# Executive Summary: Subsidy Reduction Response (2022)

## 1. Business Context
A reduction in government subsidies in 2022 created near-term margin pressure and downside risk to profitability. A cross-functional response was designed to protect earnings and stabilize liquidity.

## 2. Problem Statement
- Expected downside: 4-6% profit impact in stress quarters.
- Immediate challenge: preserve operating continuity without compromising growth.
- Decision need: prioritize interventions with measurable financial lift.

## 3. Analytical Approach
- Built a forecasting and reconciliation workflow from quarterly financial KPIs.
- Used assumption-driven scenario modeling to estimate subsidy shock and mitigation effects.
- Combined quantitative findings with SWOT priority scoring to guide execution.

## 4. Strategic Actions
- Cost optimization: targeted operating expense controls.
- Demand planning: improved forecasting discipline and pricing alignment.
- Working capital: tighter receivables and inventory control.

## 5. Results (Simulated, Calculated Assumptions)
- Profit improved from $6M (2022-Q2) to $28M (2023-Q4).
- Cash flow improved from $5M to $24M over the same period.
- Mitigation effectiveness increased from 0% (shock quarter) to 95% by 2023-Q4.
- Forecasts remained within a controlled error band while performance stabilized.
